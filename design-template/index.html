“IMARA JAMII” A SUSTAINABLE COMMUNITY

For All

About

Imara Jamii Organization is a community based organization in Mombasa, Kenya, founded in 2018. It seek to addresses youth unemployment, violent extremism and drug abuse in Mombasa. We are focused on transforming, Motivating and Empowering youths and Women so as to create an enabling employment opportunities through mentorship and entrepreneurship programmes.

Values

As the name “Imara Jamii” which is a Swahili word that means a stable society. We believe that for the community, the country and the world at large will not be able to achieve the sustainable word in an unstable society and that is we are playing a role in making a difference toward a economically and morally stable society..

Mission

Our mission is to work for the self-development, improvement and sustainability of young people and women in Mombasa through empowerment, mentorships programmes, training, and education in order to create employment, eradicate poverty, fight against drug addiction violence and radicalization.

Need

At a close number two is the coastal part of the country. This county has very high unemployment rates, even with its rich culture and what we all assume to be a healthy economy. The results of unemployment in this county are evidenced by the rise in crimes and the emergence of drug peddling that is crippling the county and rendering it’s youth unproductive and lazy. The level of literacy among the youths and women is wanting with most of them dropping out of school in their junior level of education. As a result lack of qualifications and employable skills in the long run. We have and evidential rate of crimes criminal gangs operating in every constituency consisting of youths as young as 15years.At this age, it is legally a minor who is support to be in 1st year of his high school education. Due to lack of economic stability youth often exhibit risky behaviour, including substance abuse, gang membership, aggressive and violent actions against others, stealing, vulnerability to radicalization and early pregnancy for young girls.
